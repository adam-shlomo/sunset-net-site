Safety Spec 01

The Human-in-the-Loop Mandate

Sunset Net is a decision-support tool, not an autonomous network administrator. The system generates configuration proposals. It does not execute them without your explicit, deliberate approval — and that distinction is fundamental.

Every command surfaced by Sunset Net must be reviewed, verified, and manually approved by a qualified human operator before it is sent to any device. You are the proximate cause of any change applied to your infrastructure. Sunset Net is the instrument; you are the operator.

Safety Spec 02

No Warranty of Accuracy — The Hallucination Clause

Sunset Net uses Large Language Models (LLMs) — specifically Anthropic's Claude — to generate network configuration syntax. LLMs can and do produce incorrect, non-existent, deprecated, or destructive commands. This is a known and documented limitation of the technology, not a bug unique to this product.

Generated configuration may contain syntax that is invalid for your specific platform, firmware version, or topology. It may reference interfaces, route-maps, or policy objects that do not exist in your environment. It may achieve an output that differs from your stated intent.

Sunset Net does not guarantee that any generated output will: compile without errors, apply successfully, produce the intended network state, or be free of side effects on adjacent protocols or devices.

This means your configuration metadata — including device prompts, topology descriptions, routing intent, and show command output — leaves your local environment and is transmitted to Anthropic's servers.

Anthropic's data handling is governed by their API usage policies and privacy documentation, which you should review independently. Sunset Net does not control how Anthropic stores, processes, or retains data submitted via their API.

You are solely responsible for ensuring that no sensitive personally identifiable information (PII), unencrypted credentials, authentication tokens, SNMP community strings, or proprietary customer data is included in any prompt or show command output submitted to the platform.

By using the platform, you consent to the creation and retention of these audit records.
